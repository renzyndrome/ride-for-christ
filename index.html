Where iron sharpens iron, the road bears witness.

We're rolling out before sunrise to share the open road, a hot breakfast, and a quiet moment in the Word. Built for Christian riders, designed to welcome the curious — a fellowship of brothers in Christ, building a humble and intentional riding culture rooted in faith.

Why this over Tagaytay

Tagaytay is a coffee run. Kaybiang is the South Luzon ride seasoned riders show up for — and that's the audience we're trying to reach with the "Ride with Purpose" theme.

The early sunrise stretch through Ternate sets a naturally reflective tone that makes the Word-sharing land harder than a fluorescent café ever could.

05 · Roles

Six people make it work.

Assign these before the ride. Group safety and the welcoming atmosphere both depend on roles being clear.

ROLE 01
Lead Rider
Sets pace, knows the route cold, manages convoy speed and stops.
ROLE 02
Sweep Rider
Last in formation. Stays with anyone who falls behind. No rider left alone.
ROLE 03
First-Timer Buddy
Rides alongside new riders or non-believer guests. Where the bonding actually happens.
ROLE 04
Breakfast Coordinator
Pre-books the venue or packs the breakfast. Ensures food is ready on arrival.
ROLE 05
Word Speaker
15 minutes max. Testimony format preferred over sermon. Real, not rehearsed.
ROLE 06
Documenter
Captures photo and video for content and for the next round of invites.

06 · Gear & Safety

What every rider brings.

  • Full-face or modular helmet (DOT / ECE)
  • Riding jacket with armor
  • Riding gloves
  • Closed shoes — no slippers, no sandals
  • OR/CR + driver's license
  • Enough gas to reach the next station
  • Cash for breakfast + emergency
  • Water bottle

Non-negotiables on the road.

  • Convoy formation — no breaking off
  • Hand signals followed by everyone
  • Sweep rider authority is final
  • Ride at the pace of the group
  • Mechanical issue = entire group stops

THE THEME IS THE WITNESS

How we ride is the message. A humble, disciplined, joyful convoy on the road is fellowship made visible — brothers in Christ, building a riding culture rooted in faith.
